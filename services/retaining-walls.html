A Wall Stands or Falls on What You Cannot See

Most of what makes a retaining wall last is hidden once the job is done. The base it sits on, the drainage behind it, and the way water is handled are what hold a wall in place. We studied how soil and water move on a piece of land, and that goes into the wall before the first stone is set.

  • We handle the water first because water trapped behind a wall is what pushes it over, so drainage goes in before anything gets stacked.
  • We set a proper base so the wall has solid, level ground under it instead of settling and leaning a season later.
  • We read the slope to size the wall for the load it actually has to hold back, not just the part you can see.
  • We backfill it right so the ground behind the wall drains and packs the way it should instead of working against it.

How We Build a Wall That Lasts

A wall is only as good as the work underneath it. Here is what goes into one built to hold.

1

Site and Slope Assessment

Reading the grade, the soil, and where the water runs so the wall is built for the load it carries.

2

Base Preparation

Excavating and setting a level, compacted base so the wall has solid footing from the start.

3

Drainage Installation

Putting drainage behind the wall so water moves off instead of building up and pushing it out.

4

Wall Construction and Backfill

Setting the stone or block, then backfilling so the ground behind holds and drains the right way.

How a Properly Built Wall Makes a Difference

A retaining wall is one of those jobs where cutting corners shows up later, usually after a hard rain. Doing it right means it holds for years.

  • No drainage traps water behind it until the pressure bows it out or topples it completely.
  • Bad base settles unevenly, cracks, and starts leaning within a season or two.
  • An undersized wall gives way under a slope that was always going to be too much for it.
  • A wall built right holds its line, controls the water, and protects the ground above and below it for the long haul.

Common Questions About Retaining Walls

Straight answers to the questions we hear most from property owners dealing with slopes, erosion, and ground that will not stay put.

How much does a retaining wall cost in Oklahoma?

The cost of a retaining wall depends on its length and height, the material, the slope it has to hold, and how much site and drainage work the ground needs. A short decorative wall is a very different job from a tall wall holding back a steep grade or an eroding creek bank. What matters more than the number is what goes into it, since a cheap wall built without proper drainage and a solid base usually fails and costs more to redo. We build boulder and block retaining walls across Skiatook and northeast Oklahoma and give you a clear estimate after we look at the site.

What is the best type of retaining wall for a sloped property?

It comes down to the slope, the soil, and what you want it to look like. Boulder walls handle rugged, rural ground well and stand up to weather and runoff, while segmental block gives a cleaner, finished look for yards and graded areas. Both hold ground when they are built on a proper base with drainage behind them. We work with the terrain across Osage, Tulsa, and Rogers County and match the wall to what your property actually needs.

How do I stop a hillside or creek bank from eroding?

Erosion is a water problem first. Once runoff finds a path down a slope or along a bank, it cuts in deeper every season until the ground gives way. A retaining wall, combined with the right grading and drainage, holds that soil in place and controls where the water goes. We build erosion control and creek bank stabilization walls throughout northeast Oklahoma built to slow the damage and lock the ground down.

Do retaining walls need drainage?

Yes, and it is the single most important part of a wall that lasts. Water that collects behind a wall with nowhere to go builds pressure until it pushes the wall out or knocks it over. Proper drainage behind the wall gives that water a path off your property instead of letting it work against the wall. It is one of the main reasons a wall built right outlasts one built cheap.

What areas do you build retaining walls in?

We are based near Skiatook and build retaining walls throughout Osage, Tulsa, and Rogers County, including Owasso, Collinsville, Sperry, Claremore, and the rural acreage around them. If your property sits within about 45 minutes of Skiatook and has a slope, a bank, or erosion you need held back, give us a call.
